Understanding How MUN Actually Works

This video walks through the real flow of a Model United Nations conference — from committee sessions and speeches to debates, negotiations, resolution drafting, and voting — helping you visualize how theory becomes real diplomatic practice.

Committees

Each MUN has different committees, similar to those in the real UN, such as:

  • • UNGA – United Nations General Assembly
  • • UNHRC – Human Rights Council
  • • WHO – World Health Organization
  • • UNSC – Security Council
  • • UNICEF, UNDP.

Each committee has a specific agenda (topic) to debate.

Delegates

  • • Each participant is called a delegate.
  • • You are assigned a country (not your own opinion, but that of your country’s government).
  • • You must research your country’s stance on the agenda.

Agenda (Topic)

The main issue to be discussed, e.g.:

  • • “Combating Climate Change”
  • • “Protection of Refugee Rights”
  • • “Cybersecurity Threats in Developing Nations”

Procedure of the Debate

MUNs follow a structured process:

  • a. Roll Call
    Attendance of delegates.
  • b. General Speakers’ List (GSL)
    Delegates deliver short speeches introducing their country’s view on the agenda.
  • c. Moderated Caucus
    A semi-formal discussion on subtopics, where delegates raise placards to speak.
  • d. Unmoderated Caucus
    Informal discussion — delegates move around, negotiate, and form alliances.
  • e. Draft Resolution
    Delegates form groups (blocs) to write a resolution — a formal document proposing solutions.
  • f. Voting
    Delegates vote on the resolution. The one that gets majority support passes.
  • g. Adjourned
    The committee is officially declared closed or adjourned.
