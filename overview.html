Module 1: What is the Model United Nations?

Objective: Understand the purpose, structure, and appeal of Model UN.

  • What is MUN? MUN is a simulation where students act as delegates from different countries, discussing real-world issues and proposing solutions from their assigned country’s perspective.
  • Why participate?
    • Develops skills: public speaking, diplomacy, research, negotiation, and critical thinking.
    • Opportunity to learn about world issues, meet new people, and grow as a leader.
  • How do MUN conferences work? Conferences are hosted by schools, organizations, and universities at various levels—from middle school to university.

Module 2: How Does an MUN Conference Work?

Objective: Familiarize yourself with the conference structure, the flow of debate, and committee roles.

  • Conference Registration: Teams register, receive countries and committees to represent.
  • Preparation:
    • Learn about your assigned country: geography, politics, current events.
    • Understand the topics and your committee’s focus.
  • Flow of Debate: Opening speeches, formal debate (speaker’s list), caucuses (formal/informal discussions), resolution writing, and voting.
  • Committee Roles: Delegates, Chairs (moderators), Rapporteurs (record keepers), Observers.

Module 3: Research, Rules, and Resolution Writing

Objective: Learn how to research, follow parliamentary procedure, and draft effective resolutions.

  • Country Research: Gather facts about your assigned country: demographics, economy, politics, and key alliances.
  • Topic Research: Study the issue at hand, your country’s stance, relevant UN documents, and treaties.
  • Rules of Procedure (RoP): Points (e.g., Point of Order) and Motions (e.g., move to debate, amend a resolution) help structure the debate.
  • Draft Resolutions: Collaborate with other delegates to combine ideas into a written solution to the issue. Resolutions must be clear, persuasive, and in proper format.

ACTIVITY:
Pick a country and global issue (real or imaginary). Write three facts about your country and one possible solution for your issue.

Module 4: Debate, Diplomacy, and Success at MUN

Objective: Practice public speaking, negotiation, and the art of compromise for MUN success.

  • Speaking Skills: Prepare clear, concise, and persuasive speeches; support your position with evidence and logic.
  • Negotiation: Seek alliances, listen actively, and find common ground with other delegates.
  • Consensus Building & Voting: Work towards agreements, be ready to negotiate wording for broad support. Most resolutions pass by majority but consensus is ideal.
  • Conference Etiquette: Be respectful, professional, and diplomatic at all times.
