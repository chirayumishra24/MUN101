Consensus Building & Voting

The ultimate goal in MUN is to pass resolutions that reflect broad agreement:

  • Work toward consensus: Strive to achieve language and solutions that multiple delegates can support, rather than aiming for narrow victory.
  • Compromise on wording: Small changes to phrasing or clauses can convert opposition into support.
  • Understand voting procedures: Most resolutions pass by majority vote, but securing consensus ensures stronger legitimacy and demonstrates diplomatic skill.
  • Be strategic: Knowing when to negotiate, when to speak, and when to yield your time can influence voting outcomes.

Tip: Collaborating early with sponsors and signatories can streamline consensus-building.

Conference Etiquette

Professionalism enhances credibility and respect:

  • Respect: Address all delegates politely, listen when others are speaking, and avoid interrupting.
  • Professional conduct: Dress appropriately, maintain decorum, and follow procedural rules.
  • Diplomacy: Avoid personal attacks or confrontational language. Focus on ideas, not individuals.
  • Responsiveness: Be attentive, engage in discussions, and participate actively in both formal debate and caucuses.

Tip: A diplomat’s reputation can influence allies, opponents, and even the Chair’s perception of your effectiveness.
