Chapter 2.3
How to Prepare for MUN

My Notes

TOP 6 Tips for First Timers | MUN Academy

1

Registration & Assignment

Participants can register either individually or in teams. They then give their preference for the committee and portfolio. Portfolio is the country you represent. Once the Secretariat confirms the portfolio and committee, you can start with your preparation.

2

Be aware of your country and research

When you have received your country allocation, start studying about the country. Know the country’s political system, its latest news, foreign policies and its global outlook. Your research should include:

  • What is the aim of the committee?
  • How is your country impacted and relevant to the agenda?
  • How does your country contribute to the agenda?
  • What are the efforts already made by your country to solve a given problem or situation?
  • Who are the key stakeholder countries other than your own country and what are the inter-state relations of your country with other countries? (This should include any bilateral agreements or pacts)
  • The delegates are advised to frame POIs for other delegates, to question their policies and inefficacies.

Use credible sources of information to collect data, facts and figures. If you are using statements made by other leaders, cite their original speeches.

3

Learn the plan of action

For instance, UNSC begins with opening speeches, discussion and deliberations on sub-topics, called motions, and then a Draft Resolution is signed which contains a set of solutions. Here, as a delegate you should be aware of the plan of action that your committee follows.

4

Make your opening speech

Make a speech of 1-2 minutes that introduces your country’s history with its problems, as well as the solutions it proposes for the issue being discussed. Be sure to keep your speech formal, respectful and relevant. Make sure you practice it regularly so that you’re speaking confidently. Your opening speech should set the stance your government holds in respect to the given agenda.

5

Learn the basics of MUN procedures

It is essential to understand how to make the motion, file resolutions, and also use points such as “point of order” or “point of personal privilege.” This will enable you to be able to follow the debate without becoming confused. You should be familiar with the rules of writing Position Papers, Action Papers or Draft Resolutions.

6

Practice public speaking

Talk to someone you trust or practice by talking to yourself in the mirror. This will help you develop your body communication, eye contact and clarity of voice. Although you may be shy, regular practice can help you feel more relaxed and confident.

Preparing for MUN might feel like a big task, but if you plan a little each day, you will be ready and excited to participate.

Confidence – The Real Key

MUN doesn’t mean being perfect. It’s about experimenting, learning and speaking out. It’s normal to feel anxious on your first day. Everybody starts somewhere.

To increase confidence:

  • • Stay informed and prepared.
  • • Dress well and follow the dress code of MUN.
  • • Use your placard correctly and be active in participation.
  • • Be considerate, speak clearly, and show respect.
  • • Keep calm, even if you don’t agree with somebody’s point.

Confidence in Model United Nations grows with every hour you spend engaging in the committee. The more involved you become, the more confident you will feel.

Note: Always keep your placard upright and raise it horizontally whenever needed.
