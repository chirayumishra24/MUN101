Chapter 3.2
Flow of Debate in MUN

My Notes

1

Introduction

  • Welcome: The Model UN Working Group in Ukraine introduces the session and explains its purpose.
  • Roles:
    • Chair: Moderates debate, ensures delegates speak in order.
    • Delegates: Represent countries (e.g., China, Brazil, Canada, Ukraine).
2

Roll Call

  • Delegates state their status:
    • Present → Can vote Yea, Nay, or Abstain.
    • Present and Voting → Can vote only Yea or Nay.
  • Example:
  • Brazil: Present
  • Canada: Present and Voting
3

Opening the Speakers’ List

  • Purpose: Allows delegates to formally present their position.
  • Process:
    1. The delegates raise the motion to open the Speakers’ List and set a speaking time.
    2. Delegates request to be added by raising their respective placards.
    3. Speakers’ List may be closed at any time.
4

Formal Motion Example

  • Brazil: Motion to open the Speakers’ List with 2-minute speaking time per delegate.
  • Canada: Second.
  • Chair: Motion passes. Delegates wishing to speak raise placards.
5

Speaking Example

Speeches:

  • Brazil: Proposes order of discussion — Environmental Sustainability first, followed by Space Exploration. Emphasizes urgency of biodiversity protection. Yields remaining time to Chair.
  • Canada: Supports Brazil’s focus on environmental sustainability. Yields time to China.
  • China: Agrees with Canada, stresses need for immediate global action. Yields to Chair.
  • Ukraine: Supports prioritizing environmental protection.

Questions on Positions:

  • Canada asks whether Ukraine opposes debating space exploration later.
  • Ukraine: Focus should remain on environmental sustainability.
6

Closing the Speakers’ List

  • Motion Example:
  • Brazil: The delegate of Brazil would like to raise a motion to close the Speaker’s List
  • Voting: The delegates vote in favour or against the motion, if there is a majority, the motion passes.
  • Chair: Motion passes.

Note: Delegates must yield remaining time after speaking.

7

Agenda Setting

  • Motion Example:
  • Canada: Motion to set agenda: Environmental Sustainability first, then Space Exploration.
  • Chair: Motion passes.
  • Debate begins on Environmental Sustainability.
  • Next agenda topics require a motion to proceed.
8

Moderated Caucus

Definition & Process:

  • Definition: Structured debate with set speaking times.
  • Process:
    1. Delegate motions for a Moderated Caucus.
    2. The chair calls on delegates one by one.
    3. Requires subtopic, speaking time, and total duration.

Example:

  • Canada: The delegate of Canada would like to raise a motion to move into a moderated caucus on the topic “Environment Sustainability with special emphasis on Sustainable Development Goals” with per speaker time being 60 seconds and total time being 1 hour.
  • Voting in favour of the motion or against, if there is a majority in votes, the motion passes.
  • Chair: Motion passes

Note: The committee can move into either a moderated or unmoderated caucus at any time. It also is not necessary to begin with a moderated caucus first.

9

Unmoderated Caucus

  • Definition: Informal debate. Delegates may move freely, discuss privately, draft resolutions, or take breaks.
  • Process Example:
    • Brazil: Motion for 15-minute Unmoderated Caucus to discuss Draft Resolution 1.0.
    • Canada: Second. Motion passes.
    • Delegates collaborate freely.
    • Extension motion: Ukraine moves to add 5 minutes; seconded by Brazil. Motion passes.

Note: Even though the delegates are allowed to move freely, debate and discuss, the delegates should not indulge in any unparliamentary behaviour as they are still being awarded marks for their negotiation and convincing skills.The delegates are advised to engage in discussions and talk to other delegates regarding their concerns and beliefs.

10

Points

Points do not affect committee flow directly:

  • Point of Inquiry: Clarify procedure or topic. Example: “How much time remains?&rdquo,“What is the order of speeches?”;
  • Point of Personal Privilege: Related to personal comfort (e.g., adjust microphone, leave room).
  • Point of Information: Ask for clarification on draft resolution or topic. These are also raised to question other delegates during their speeches.
  • Point of Order:Correct factual mistakes in the speeches of other delegates or in case of procedural faults.
11

Draft Resolutions

  • Delegates draft resolutions during caucuses.
  • Amendments:
    • Friendly: Authors agree → added automatically.
    • Unfriendly: Authors disagree → requires committee vote.
  • Example:
    • Brazil motions an unfriendly amendment to exempt certain substances from classification.
    • Chair calls vote → Amendment passes and is added.
  • Right of Reply: Delegates may respond if their country is criticized.
  • Withdrawing a Resolution: If an unfriendly amendment is unacceptable, a country may withdraw the resolution, negotiate, revise, and reintroduce it.
12

Voting Procedures

  • A motion to enter the voting procedure is required before voting.
  • Two-for, Two-against debate may occur before final vote.
  • Roll call voting: Delegates called individually, vote Yea, Nay, or Abstain.
  • Delegates who were present and voting at roll call cannot abstain on substantive votes.
  • Example: Roll call vote on draft resolution Sanctions on Venezuela.
    • Brazil → Yea
    • China → Nay
    • Ukraine → Yea
    • Canada → Yea
13

Suspending or Ending Committee

  • Suspending session: Motion to pause committee (e.g., lunch).
  • Adjourn debate: Motion to end debate on current topic.
  • Adjourn committee: Motion to end the entire session.
  • Examples of motions passed:
    • Suspend until the next meeting → passes.
    • Move to the next agenda (Space Exploration) → passes.
    • Adjourn committee entirely → passes.
