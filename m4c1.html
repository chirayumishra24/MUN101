Communication & Diplomacy in Model United Nations

How to Structure the Speech?

Speaking Skills

Effective communication is the cornerstone of MUN. Delegates should:

  • Prepare clear and concise speeches: Organize ideas logically, focusing on one topic or point at a time. Avoid long, convoluted statements.
  • Persuade with evidence: Support arguments with facts, statistics, historical examples, or international law. This gives credibility and shows your understanding of the issue.
  • Structure your speech:
    1. Introduction: State your country and position.
    2. Body: Present evidence, explain reasoning, and propose solutions.
    3. Conclusion: Summarize key points and emphasize action or recommendation.
  • Delivery matters: Speak confidently, maintain eye contact, use clear tone and volume, and pace your speech to allow the audience to absorb information.

Tip: Practice speeches beforehand to reduce nervousness and enhance clarity.

Negotiation

Negotiation is a critical skill in MUN, as most resolutions require collaboration:

  • Seek alliances: Identify delegates with similar positions or interests. Forming coalitions increases the likelihood of support for your proposals.
  • Listen actively: Understand other delegates’ positions and concerns. This allows you to propose compromises that satisfy multiple parties.
  • Flexibility: Be ready to adjust your proposals or language without compromising your country’s core stance. Negotiation is about finding win-win solutions.
  • Persuasive diplomacy: Use tact, respect, and logic to influence others without confrontation. Avoid aggressive or dismissive language.
